# Reaper Six — Book One Synopsis

**Series:** Task Force 154: The Reapers  
**Subtitle:** Book One  
**Setting:** Fallujah, Iraq — November 2004 through early 2005  
**Word count:** ~45,000 words  
**Pages (KDP 6x9):** 147

---

## LOGLINE

Six off-the-books operators in Fallujah discover that the targeting list they've been running isn't about weapons — it's about eliminating the people who can prove what the weapons weren't.

---

## PREMISE

Late 2004. Commander Marcus Hale arrives at an unmarked forward operating base outside Fallujah with a rifle, a ruck, and a dog that doesn't officially exist. He has been assigned to lead Task Force 154 — a classified six-man unit operating outside every official record, running targeting packages through a handler they've never met under authorization that nobody will acknowledge.

Their first package arrives at 2100 on a Tuesday. By Wednesday morning, they have begun to suspect it is not what it claims to be.

---

## STRUCTURE: PROLOGUE + 28 CHAPTERS + EPILOGUE

### PROLOGUE — "The Accounting"
**Location:** An unmarked facility, Northern Virginia — February 2005 (six months after the main events)

Hale sits across a table from a man in his fifties — gray at the temples, wedding ring, shoes that need resoling. The man asks questions. Hale has a practiced answer and a true answer. He has decided he is done with practiced answers. The prologue is a frame: it tells us Hale survived, that there was something to account for, and that he intends to tell it honestly.

### ACT ONE: THE UNIT ASSEMBLES (Chapters 1–7)

**Ch1 — "Meridian":** Hale arrives at the FOB at 0340. Brick meets him at the door — assessing, withholding judgment. Ares is released from his crate. The terminal is already installed. The authorization documents are more thorough and more hollow than anything Hale has seen.

**Ch2 — "The Parish Delivers":** Padre and Gremlin arrive mid-argument, still going. The team begins to take shape. Brick positions himself at Hale's left and slightly behind. Gremlin fixes the generator. Padre cooks from contractor-issue rations and produces something the team eats without complaint. Cricket studies the terminal.

**Ch3 — "Pattern of Life":** Cricket's POV. He reads the first package twice — once for intelligence, once for something he doesn't have a professional term for. He types four words in his log. The pen stops clicking.

**Ch4 — "Ares Works":** First field movement. Ares on point. The operational chemistry of the unit begins to establish itself. Wraith is already where he needs to be before anyone positions him.

**Ch5 — "The First Confession":** Padre sits outside with his rosary and Ares sits beside him — neither leashed nor commanded, simply present. Gremlin draws a ghost on his rifle stock. Cricket's four words are referenced but not revealed.

**Ch6 — "Reaper Six, Execute":** First operation. The team executes Package 1 cleanly. Hale reads the biography section afterward and files a question he doesn't yet have language for.

**Ch7 — "What Brick Knows":** Brick tells Hale what he's been observing. Not aggressively — as information. Hale understands he is being measured and measures back.

### ACT TWO: THE LIST BUILDS (Chapters 8–15)

**Ch8–10:** Packages 2 through 4. Cricket's pen stops more frequently. The pattern he is identifying is not in any single package — it's in what the packages have in common, and what they don't.

**Ch11 — "The Green Zone":** Brick travels to Baghdad independently. He asks about methodology, operational history, whether the unit that preceded them worked under the same informational constraints. The answer is yes. He brings this back in the form of five words at the next briefing.

**Ch12–14:** Packages 5 through 6. The Confessor's phrasing shifts subtly. Cricket notes it. He has underlined three things in a biography section and put the folded paper in his boot.

**Ch15 — "Two Packages. Fourteen Days.":** The Architect signals the final phase. Two packages remaining in fourteen days. Cricket shares what he has built. The team sits with it. Nobody leaves the room with the same understanding they came in with.

### ACT THREE: THE DECISION (Chapters 16–24)

**Ch16 — "The Picture" (The Following Morning):** The team assembles around what Cricket has assembled. Hale sees the architecture. The list was never about insurgents. It was never about weapons proliferation. It was built before the war started, by someone who needed to know what the intelligence actually showed — and needed to make sure the people who knew couldn't say so.

**Ch17–19:** The team works the problem. Brick's Green Zone confirmation. Padre's theological framing of what they've been doing. Gremlin asks the most important question anybody asks in the entire series. Wraith says nothing and is never in the wrong place.

**Ch20 — "GREY" (Two Days Later):** GREY contact. The oversight mechanism that isn't. GREY acknowledges the seventh package issue: "I told you to look carefully at the seventh package before deciding what to do with it. You looked. You did not run it. That was the correct decision."

**Ch21 — "The Video Call":** The Architect requests live video interface for the first time. Hale performs. The Architect performs. Two performances, perfectly matched, producing a conversation entirely false and completely informative.

**Ch22 — "What We Have":** The team has established that the list predates the unit, that the authorization structure is self-authorizing, and that what they've been running is not what they were told. The question becomes: what do they do with that.

**Ch23 — "Kassem":** Package 7. The team's last clean execution. They know what they know. They run it anyway. The moral weight of that choice is not dramatized — it is noted, filed, and carried.

**Ch24 — "The Forty-Eight Hours":** The countdown to Farhat. The team prepares. Cricket's pen doesn't stop. Wraith begins building something nobody asks about.

### ACT FOUR: FARHAT (Chapters 25–28 + Epilogue)

**Ch25 — "The Khalil Context":** Padre's section. His history with operations of this kind, specifically the Khalil operation. The morning after that operation, he had built a picture that pointed at a thing he did not want to be true. He ran it anyway. He is doing it again.

**Ch26 — "The Evidence" (Overnight, After Farhat):** Cricket has been the third man to understand the room on the night TF154 assembled. He has been running a parallel thread. What he has now — the full picture, the documentation, the record — is something that needs to survive regardless of what happens next.

**Ch27 — "What Brick Sends":** Brick sends six words. The completion update will not come. The team is no longer running the list — they are figuring out what to do with having run it.

**Ch28 — "The Last Night":** Wraith plants a cached drive. Location known only to Wraith. The team does the work of people who understand they may be the last people to hold this particular information. Hale leads them to the vehicle. He does not look back.

**Epilogue:** Ares waits by the vehicle, facing the door. The frame closes. We return to the unmarked facility in Northern Virginia. Hale has given his true answer. The man across the table writes something down. The coffee has been cold since before they started.

---

## CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION

What do you do when the machine that built you starts pointing you at the wrong targets — and you're the only ones in a position to know?

---

## ENDING

TF154 does not blow the whistle. They do not go public. They do not confront The Architect directly. What they do is more dangerous to the people who built them: they document it. Cricket's record. Wraith's drive. The four words that were typed the night the first package arrived.

The work continues. So do they.
