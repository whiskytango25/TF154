## UNIT DESIGNATIONS

### Task Force 154
- Does not officially exist
- Funded through three layers of defense contractor shell companies
- Authorization: a document that has since been destroyed
- Staffed by operators too good to cut and too difficult to manage inside normal command structures
- Internal callsign system: Reaper Six is the unit, individual callsigns are operator-specific
- No patch, no flag, no chain of command anyone will acknowledge — Book 1

### NSW — Naval Special Warfare
- The broader community TF154 operators come from
- Not named directly in tactical ops; implied by trident usage on patch

### Reaper Six
- The unit's operating callsign in Book 1
- Becomes the series title for Book 1

## THE COMMAND ARCHITECTURE

### The Parish
- The organizational layer between The Architect and the unit
- Delivers targeting packages through The Confessor channel
- Not a person — an entity with a budget line nobody will audit

### The Confessor
- The analyst interface — female, voice only in Book 1
- Delivers packages through a specific terminal channel
- Her phrasing shifts subtly between packages 4 and 5 (Cricket notices)
- Real function differs from stated function — developed in Book 2

### The Architect
- The unit's handler
- NSC-adjacent
- "Not his real name"
- Runs the authorization structure that terminates at its own source
- First video contact: Ch21, Book 1

### GREY
- Internal oversight mechanism, or what claims to be one
- Contacts the unit during the seventh package window
- "The briefs are wrong too often to be wrong" — Brick's line, Book 2

### The Authorization Structure
- Simultaneously thorough and hollow
- Tells you exactly how the machine runs without telling you who built it or why
- Review code terminates at its own source — self-authorizing loop
- The document that created TF154 has since been destroyed

## OPERATIONAL TERMINOLOGY

### Package
- TF154's internal term for a targeting assignment
- Arrives with a biography section, operational history, and target profile
- Cricket reads each package twice
- Book 1 runs 8 packages total; "Package 8 of 8" is Farhat (final)
- "The list was never about weapons. It was about the people who could prove what the weapons weren't."

### Pattern of Life
- Intelligence methodology: documenting a target's routine, contacts, movements
- Used to establish targeting validity
- Cricket uses this methodology in Ch3 in a secondary sense — the pattern in the packages themselves

### The Column
- Brick's shorthand for an assessment being complete
- "The column was full" = he had confirmed everything he went to confirm; the decision was not his to make

### Disponibilidad
- Spanish: availability
- Padre's grandmother's word
- The willingness to be found — a disposition, not a state
- Theological concept repurposed for operational context

### Four Words (Cricket's Log)
- Typed the night after the first package arrived
- Never added to across all six books
- Never revealed to the reader
- The record exists; what it says is a permanent open question

## LOCATIONS

### The FOB (Forward Operating Base)
- Unmarked, outside Fallujah
- Converted agricultural structure
- Metal door — new, security-minded, recently installed
- One terminal: hardwired cable, origin not documented in any site survey
- Generator: ran rough until Gremlin fixed it
- Smells of generator fuel
- 200 meters from the landing strip

### Fallujah / Jolan District
- Primary operational area, Book 1
- The Second Battle of Fallujah (Operation Phantom Fury): November–December 2004
- TF154 operates in the margins and aftermath of Phantom Fury — concurrent and following, not after its conclusion
- The Jolan district is the most contested area of Phantom Fury
- Concrete block construction typical of Iraqi urban architecture of the era

### Anbar Province
- The broader theater — flat, open, featureless desert
- Sandy tan — Anbar Province Iraqi desert floor; not rocky, not dunes
- Sparse low scrub brush at ground level
- Dark packed earth in the immediate FOB vicinity

### Baghdad Green Zone
- Ch11: Brick travels here independently
- Where he asks about the prior unit's methodology
- Where he gets the answer yes

### Mosul
- Ch20: GREY contact location
- Different building from previous interface locations — notable

### An Unmarked Facility, Northern Virginia
- The Prologue and Epilogue frame location
- February 2005 — six months after the main events
- The accounting takes place here

### An Unnamed Building (Farhat Operation)
- "A building not on any operational map"
- Ch25 final operation location

## EQUIPMENT AND KIT

### The Terminal
- Single hardwired terminal at the FOB
- Budget that the building itself would never suggest
- Video capability confirmed (Ch1) but not used until Ch21
- The cable runs to an origin not documented in any site survey

### The Patch (TF154 — Book 1)
- IR subdued US flag
- SEAL trident centered over it
- No unit designation, no text, no color
- Hook-backed
- Visible on FLIR; anonymous to everyone else
- "The patch that says everything to the right people and nothing to everyone else"

### The Challenge Coin (TF154 — Book 1)
- Antique brass, 1.75 inch
- SEAL trident obverse
- Blank reverse
- "A challenge coin with nothing on the back is its own kind of message"

### Ares's Equipment
- No leash. No collar. No harness hardware.
- Moves freely.
- Hard-shell kennel for transit (Hale carried it himself, four flights)

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### Operation Phantom Fury
- Largest urban combat operation by US forces since Hue City (1968)
- November–December 2004, Fallujah
- TF154 operates in its margins — not after its conclusion, concurrent with and following
- The Marines and soldiers who took that city building by building are the historical anchor

### LOGCAP
- Logistics Civil Augmentation Program
- Defense contractor logistics framework
- The shell companies funding TF154 exist within this ecosystem

### AMC (Air Mobility Command)
- Military air transport referenced in backstory
- 463L pallets (standard AMC cargo pallets) — operational texture reference

### Iraq Theater 2004
- Burn pits: standard waste disposal on forward operating bases
- Contractor flights: Beechcraft-type aircraft with logos deliberately obscured
- Contractor vehicle wear: not military maintenance, not civilian neglect — the intermediate state of equipment belonging to a budget line nobody audits
- MRE rations: contractor-issue; taste like a committee decided what food was, described it to someone who'd never eaten
