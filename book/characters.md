## CORE UNIT — TF154

---

### COMMANDER MARCUS HALE
**Callsign:** Cinco  
**Rank:** LTJG (Lieutenant Junior Grade, O-2) at unit stand-up  
**Role:** Team leader, direct action  
**Books:** 1–6 (survivor)

**Physical:**
- 6'1", athletic build
- Face that surveillance cameras have genuine difficulty resolving clearly
- Forgettable in a crowd, impossible to forget once you've been in the room with him
- Trained in classical Japanese martial arts before he ever picked up a rifle (katana is canonical — referenced but not dramatized in Book 1)

**Kit (Book 1):**
- Full multicam battle rattle, plate carrier
- Tactical helmet with NODs flipped up
- Dark skull balaclava — fabric, skull pattern visible but tactical, not bright or glowing
- M4 rifle with suppressor, slung on two-point sling across chest
- Tanto-style fixed blade on right hip
- Carries his own gear; carried Ares's crate off every aircraft himself

**Cognitive register:**
- Architectural. Builds frameworks, reads rooms, processes threat geometry into philosophical structure
- Dark humor as armor — gets quieter under pressure, not louder
- Processes the authorization structure the way an engineer reads blueprints: looking for what the design reveals about the designer
- Has a practiced answer and a true answer. Is done with practiced answers.

**Key traits:**
- Exceptional in a way that costs the people around him — he doesn't fully see this in Book 1
- Reads silences as information
- Does not arrive anywhere in a hurry because arriving somewhere in a hurry usually means you hadn't thought enough about it
- Chose Ares — or more accurately, understood that Ares chose him and honored it

**Arc across series:**
- Book 1: Discovers what he's been part of; chooses documentation over confrontation
- Books 2–5: Escalating cost of his exceptionalism on the unit
- Book 6: Survivor. What survives of him is the question the series ends on.

**Relationship to Brick:** The productive friction of two men who respect each other without being required to say so. Brick takes measure; Hale is measured. The rank exchange in Ch1 establishes the register: "I'm an E-9, Lieutenant. Brick's a title, not a courtesy."

### MASTER CHIEF PETTY OFFICER DARNELL OKAFOR
**Callsign:** Brick  
**Rank:** MCPO (E-9)  
**Role:** Machine gunner, unit backbone  
**Books:** 1–6 (survivor)

**Physical:**
- 6'3", 245 lbs
- Fills a doorframe at 0340 in a way that personnel files do not prepare you for
- Positioned at Hale's left and slightly behind — this becomes his default geography

**Cognitive register:**
- Declarative. Files, judges, compresses. Subject-verb-object. No subordinate clauses.
- Thinks in units of judgment and consequence, not philosophical architecture
- "North." One word. The whole decision.
- His silences are not voids to be filled — they are positions being taken

**Key traits:**
- Twenty years minimum. Has been doing this long enough that his pattern recognition is structural
- Can tell the difference between a guard who changes his routine and a guard who was told to be somewhere at a specific time
- Knows when Hale is done talking and when he isn't — files this as a data point
- Traveled to the Baghdad Green Zone independently in Ch11 to verify what he suspected
- His most important line (not yet said in Book 1): "The briefs are wrong too often to be wrong."
- Has someone named Maya (Book 2 thread — not developed in Book 1)

**Voice:**
- Short sentences. Hard units.
- "They were." — two words as a complete briefing
- "The list predates us." — the most loaded statement in the book delivered as a logistical observation

### SENIOR CHIEF PETTY OFFICER TOMMY VASQUEZ
**Callsign:** Padre  
**Rank:** SCPO (E-8)  
**Role:** Medic and breacher  
**Books:** 1–6 (KIA Book 6)

**Physical:**
- Medical professional's hands — precise, trained to operate under conditions that would make other hands shake
- These are not a breacher's hands, which Hale files as a question for later

**Background:**
- Trained for the priesthood, left — approximately eleven years before Book 1
- Theological structure repurposed for tactical questions
- His grandmother called it *disponibilidad* — availability, the willingness to be found

**Kit:**
- Carries a rosary and det cord on the same vest
- Names every door he blows — this is not explained and does not need to be

**Cognitive register:**
- Theological precision applied to practical things
- Makes exact distinctions — doesn't linger on atmosphere
- Occupies his ambiguity rather than resolving it
- Sees the desert as witness, not as having opinions
- Takes confessions he has no authority to hear

**Key traits:**
- Cooks. First deployment, someone has to feed the team, and Padre decided it would be him.
- The MRE component + camp stove transformation is one of his defining scenes
- His history with the Khalil operation (referenced in Ch25) explains his capacity to run packages he suspects are wrong

**Relationship to Ares:**
- They sat outside together Ch5 — the man with his rosary and the dog with his sovereignty
- The most companionable silence Padre experienced at the FOB. He noted it. Filed it.

### PETTY OFFICER FIRST CLASS JIN-HO YOON
**Callsign:** Cricket  
**Rank:** PO1 (E-6)  
**Role:** Sniper and intelligence  
**Books:** 1–6 (KIA Book 6)

**Physical:**
- Pen-stop clicking is the involuntary tell in an otherwise controlled system
- Reads left to right, systematically
- Weight shifts slightly when something changes in a room

**Cognitive register:**
- Pattern recognition and anomaly detection — analytical, systematic
- Self-diagnostics in technical language: "He recognized it, noted it, applied the standard countermeasure: the picture was not complete. Incomplete pictures required more work before they required responses. Continue."
- Relabels emotional responses in operational categories — he didn't call the necessary adjustment an accommodation; he called it logistics
- Thinks in terms of compatibility questions, not metaphors

**Key traits:**
- Reads each package twice — once for intelligence, once for something he doesn't have a professional term for
- How a targeting package represents a person: the compression of a life into the details useful for determining whether it should continue
- The four words typed the night the first package arrived — never added to, never revealed
- By the end of Book 1, he has built a complete record. It is the most dangerous thing in Iraq.

**The pen:**
- When it stops clicking, something is wrong
- By November 2004, it stops clicking a lot

### PETTY OFFICER FIRST CLASS DESMOND OKELLO
**Callsign:** Wraith  
**Rank:** PO1 (E-6)  
**Role:** Communications and direct action  
**Books:** 1–6 (survivor)

**Physical:** Not extensively described — this is intentional

**Key traits:**
- Arrived at the FOB before the team. Nobody saw him come in.
- Positions himself correctly in every moving formation without being told, without discussing it
- Says almost nothing across the entire book — every word carries weight
- No POV sections — his interiority is access-restricted by design
- His silence is a feature, not a gap

**Critical action (Ch28):**
- Plants a cached drive
- Location known only to Wraith
- Matters in Book 5

**Relationship to unit:**
- Present without announcing himself
- Geography in a room is information; Wraith treats his own position as tactical
- The most dangerous person in TF154 that nobody writes threat assessments about

### CHIEF PETTY OFFICER RORY MACTAVISH
**Callsign:** Gremlin  
**Rank:** CPO (E-7)  
**Role:** Weapons and vehicles  
**Books:** 1–6 (KIA Book 6)

**Physical:**
- The easiest expression in any room
- Assessing eyes that do considerably more work than the easy expression above them

**Key traits:**
- Fixed the generator the first afternoon — appeared outside with a tool roll, 20 minutes, clean
- Drew a ghost on his rifle stock in Week 1 — never explained
- Produced a camp kettle from his kit; drinks tea with the expression of a man who has earned it
- Asks the most important question anybody asks in the entire series (not specified — this is intentional)
- His best lines land harder because 30% of his other dialogue is rough, throwaway, unfinished

**Voice:**
- His dialogue is in the right register throughout
- Exit lines are often the sharpest — this is correct, the contrast makes them sharp
- Genuinely enjoys his work in the way of someone who has found the exact job they were built for

### ARES
**Designation:** K9, Belgian Malinois / black German Shepherd cross  
**Status:** Retires alive, approximately 2007 (Book 4 era)

**Physical:**
- 85 pounds
- Black fur — absorbs light; in darkness he disappears except for the amber eyes
- Amber eyes: natural reflection, not glowing — they catch firelight the way a working dog's eyes do
- Mouth closed when working. Always.
- No false alerts on record. Not once.
- No leash, no collar — moves freely

**Origin:**
- Does not officially exist — no manifest, no records
- Hale carried his crate himself off every aircraft in the transit chain. Four flights. Turned down two offers of help.
- Chose Hale on the third day at his prior assignment — walked away from his assigned handler and sat at Hale's left knee. Nobody explained it. Nobody needed to.

**Behavior:**
- Always on Hale's LEFT side, slightly ahead — not because he was trained to be there but because he decided to be there
- Ears forward and rotating, reading the environment continuously
- Works in the posture of something that has done its job and is not done working
- Neither runs, nor barks, nor shows fear in any documented field scenario
- When Ares is calm, everything is fine. When Ares goes still — not relaxed still, working still — pay attention.

**Relationship with Padre:**
- Sat outside together the first week, the man with his rosary and the dog with his sovereignty
- The most companionable silence Padre experienced at the FOB

**Series fate:**
- Retires alive ~2007 — his own scene, not dramatized as a loss
- Never killed, never seriously injured in combat

## COMMAND STRUCTURE / HANDLERS

---

### THE ARCHITECT
**Real name:** Unknown (appears once in the series — not in Book 1)  
**Role:** Unit handler  
**Designation:** NSC-adjacent  

- Voice on a line — no visual until Ch21 (Book 1)
- Ch21: requests live video interface for the first time; performs the conversation as carefully as Hale does
- Authorizes the list through The Parish
- Built the authorization structure that is simultaneously thorough and hollow
- "The kind of location that existed because it needed to exist and which nobody was going to acknowledge after he left"
- His interest in Hale predates Book 1 — the Ramadi incident (2003) is why he selected Hale

---

### THE CONFESSOR
**Real name:** Unknown  
**Role:** Parish analyst interface  
**Gender:** Female (voice only, Book 1)  

- Intentionally underdeveloped in Book 1 — series architecture decision
- Becomes active in Book 2
- Packages are delivered through her channel
- Her phrasing shifts subtly between packages — Cricket notices

---

### THE PARISH
**Nature:** Organizational layer between The Architect and the unit  
**Function:** Target package delivery system

- Not a person — an entity
- Three layers of defense contractor shell companies serve as funding cover
- The review code terminates at its own source

---

### GREY
**Nature:** Internal oversight mechanism — or what claims to be one  
**Function:** Ostensibly monitors operational compliance

- Contacts the unit during the seventh package decision window (Ch20)
- Initial signal: suggested running the seventh package
- Corrected in editorial pass: "I told you to look carefully at the seventh package before deciding what to do with it. You looked. You did not run it. That was the correct decision."
