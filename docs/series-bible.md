---

**Series title:** Task Force 154: The Reapers  
**Pen name:** Jack Mercer  
**Publisher:** Whiskey Tango Industries LLC (Texas)  
**Total books:** 6 + prequel novel  
**Timeline:** Iraq 2004–2010, Afghanistan 2010  
**Genre:** Military historical fiction / conspiracy thriller  

---

## PREMISE

Task Force 154 does not officially exist.

It is funded through three layers of defense contractor shell companies, authorized by a document that has since been destroyed, and staffed by operators who were too good to cut and too difficult to manage inside normal command structures.

They receive targeting packages through an entity called The Parish. Their handler is a man who calls himself The Architect. They operate in Iraq during the Global War on Terror — off every official record, visible to no oversight body, accountable to no chain of command anyone will acknowledge.

They are called the Reapers. They are very good at their work.

The series is not about war. It is about the distance between what institutions say they are and what they do — and about the people who operate in that distance and have to decide, every day, what they are going to be.

---

## TONE AND REGISTER

- **Not an action series.** Escalates morally, not kinetically.
- **Slow institutional dread** is the primary atmosphere. The horror is procedural.
- **Dark humor as armor.** The team's humor is real and specific — not performed, not comic relief. It is the way people under sustained moral pressure stay functional.
- **Prose style:** Deliberate, architecturally constructed. Dense but not difficult. Every sentence does work.
- **No omniscient narrator.** POV is always anchored in a character's consciousness, usually Hale.
- **The system is the antagonist** — not a person, not an enemy combatant. The mechanism that TF154 is part of is what the series is about.

---

## THEMES

1. **Institutional complicity** — how good people are used by systems they don't fully understand
2. **The ethics of execution** — what it means to be very good at work with moral weight
3. **Trust inside sealed systems** — who do you trust when there's no external verification
4. **The cost of exceptionalism** — Hale's capability costs the people around him
5. **Documentation as witness** — Jack Mercer's camera; Cricket's logs; the record that survives
6. **What the unit is vs. what it was told it is** — the list was never about weapons

## SETTING

**Primary theater:** Fallujah and surrounding Anbar Province, Iraq — November 2004 through 2010  
**Secondary locations:** Baghdad Green Zone, Mosul, Afghanistan (Book 6 finale)

**Historical anchor:** The Second Battle of Fallujah (Operation Phantom Fury) runs November–December 2004. TF154 operates in the margins and aftermath of Phantom Fury — not after its conclusion but concurrent with and following it. The Jolan district is their primary operational area in Book 1.

**The FOB:** Unmarked forward operating base, Fallujah outer districts. Converted agricultural structure. One terminal with a hardwired cable whose origin is not documented. Metal door, new, security-minded. Generator that runs rough until Gremlin fixes it. Smells of generator fuel. The burn pit works somewhere upwind.

**Environmental texture:**
- Burn pit smell — diesel, plastic, what the day produced that needed to disappear
- Sandy packed Iraqi desert — Anbar Province, flat, featureless, not rocky
- Contractor vehicles with half-sanded logos
- FOB material poverty — dried rations that taste like a committee decided what food was
- Orange glow of other units' burn pits on the horizon at 0100

## WORLD-BUILDING RULES

**The unit structure:**
- No official designation outside internal use
- Target packages arrive as numbered items through The Parish / Confessor channel
- Authorization chain: Architect → Parish → Confessor → unit terminal
- The review code terminates at its own source — self-authorizing loop
- GREY is the internal oversight mechanism that isn't

**The packages:**
- Each target arrives as a "package" with a biography section
- Cricket reads each package twice — once for intelligence, once for something he doesn't have a professional term for
- The packages are numbered sequentially. "Package 8 of 8" means Farhat is the last
- The list was built before the war started

**The terminal:**
- Single hardwired terminal at the FOB
- Video capability confirmed in Book 1 but not used until The Architect requests it in Ch21
- Cable runs to an origin not documented in any site survey

**The authorization structure:**
- Thorough and hollow simultaneously
- Tells you exactly how the machine runs without telling you who built it or why
- The document that authorized TF154 has since been destroyed

**The Parish / The Confessor:**
- The Confessor is the analyst interface — female, voice only in Book 1
- The Parish is the organizational layer between The Architect and the unit
- Intentionally thin in Book 1; becomes active in Book 2
- Her real function is not what the unit is told it is

**The Architect:**
- NSC-adjacent
- Not his real name
- His real name is not revealed until later in the series
- Runs video interface for the first time in Ch21 (Book 1)

**GREY:**
- Internal oversight mechanism
- Contradiction in Book 1: initially suggests running the seventh package; later acknowledges not running it was correct
- Fixed phrasing: "I told you to look carefully at the seventh package before deciding what to do with it. You looked. You did not run it. That was the correct decision."

## SERIES ARCHITECTURE — LOCKED DETAILS

**Ares:**
- Retires alive approximately 2007 (Book 4 era)
- Never killed, never injured beyond field conditions
- His retirement is its own quiet scene

**The four words (Cricket's log):**
- Typed once in Ch3, read Ch5/Ch10/Ch16
- Never added to, never revealed within the series
- What they are is a permanent open question

**The ghost on the rifle stock:**
- Drawn by Gremlin, Ch5
- Referenced Ch27
- Never explained across six books

**Wraith's cached drive:**
- Planted Ch28
- Location known only to Wraith
- Matters in Book 5

**Brick's most important line (not yet said in Book 1):**
- "The briefs are wrong too often to be wrong."

**Book 6 KIAs:** Padre, Cricket, Gremlin, Dallas, Ragnarok  
**Book 6 survivors:** Hale, Brick, Wraith, Jack
