## SECTION 2 — THE HOOK

Late 2004. Fallujah.

Six operators. No records. No patch. No chain of command anyone will acknowledge.

Their targets arrive as packages. Their handler is a voice on a line. Their authorization is a document that has since been destroyed.

Task Force 154 does not officially exist. It is funded through three layers of defense contractor shell companies and staffed by operators who were too good to cut and too difficult to manage inside normal command structures.

They are called the Reapers.

When Commander Marcus Hale arrives at an unmarked forward operating base with nothing but a rifle, a ruck, and a dog that doesn't officially exist, he expects the mission to be what it claims to be. It takes one package for him to understand it isn't.

*The list was never about weapons. It was about the people who could prove what the weapons weren't.*

## SECTION 3 — THE STORY

### WHAT THIS BOOK IS

Reaper Six is a military historical fiction novel set in Iraq during the Global War on Terror, 2004-2005. It follows Commander Marcus Hale and the five operators of Task Force 154 — an off-the-books SEAL unit running black operations through a handler they've never met and an authorization structure nobody can trace.

It is not a war story. It is a story about what happens when the machine that built you starts pointing you at the wrong targets — and what six very good operators decide to do about it.

The unit is small. The stakes are not.

### THE UNIT

**Commander Marcus Hale.** Team leader. Forgettable face, surveillance cameras have trouble with him. Trained in classical Japanese martial arts before he ever picked up a rifle. Arrived at the FOB with a rifle, a ruck, and a dog that doesn't officially exist.

**Master Chief Darnell Okafor — Brick.** Machine gunner. Speaks in complete sentences when others use paragraphs. Has been at the forward operating base three days when Hale arrives and has formed a comprehensive negative opinion of the incoming officer.

**Senior Chief Tommy Vasquez — Padre.** Medic and breacher. Carries a rosary and det cord on the same vest. Names every door he blows. Takes confessions he has no authority to hear.

**Petty Officer First Class Jin-ho Yoon — Cricket.** Sniper and intelligence. His pen stops clicking when he finds something wrong. By November 2004 it stops clicking a lot.

**Petty Officer First Class Desmond Okello — Wraith.** Communications and direct action. Arrived before the team did. Nobody saw him come in.

**Chief Petty Officer Rory MacTavish — Gremlin.** Weapons and vehicles. Loudest, most inadvisable, most genuinely fun person on the team. First week: drew a ghost on his rifle stock, fixed the generator, and asked the most important question anybody asks in the entire series.

And **Ares.** Belgian Malinois and black German Shepherd cross. 85 pounds. Amber eyes. No false alerts on record. Unofficially: the most documented K9 in the theater. Officially: does not exist.

### THE ARCHITECTURE

TF154 receives targeting packages through an entity called The Parish, run by an analyst who calls herself The Confessor. Their handler is a man who calls himself The Architect. Authorization flows one direction. The review code terminates at its own source.

The first package is clean. The second package has the same shape as the first. By the third package, Cricket has underlined three things in a biography section and put the folded paper in his boot.

By the seventh package, they know what they've been running.

The list isn't about insurgents. It isn't about weapons proliferation. It was built before the war started, by someone who needed to know what the intelligence actually showed — and needed to make sure the people who knew couldn't say so.

*The work continues. So do they.*
