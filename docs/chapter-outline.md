# Reaper Six — Chapter-by-Chapter Outline

**Format:** Chapter title / Location / Dateline / Key beats  
**Note:** Chapter datelines use relative time markers per editorial pass (no Ch-number references)

---

## PROLOGUE — "The Accounting"
**Location:** An Unmarked Facility, Northern Virginia  
**Dateline:** February 2005 — Six months after the events of the novel

**Beats:**
- Hale sits across a table from a man in his fifties: gray at the temples (earned), wedding ring, shoes that need resoling
- The coffee went cold twenty minutes ago; nobody offered to replace it — information
- Hale has been on three aircraft to get here, none carrying his name on the manifest
- The room is in no country he has been told the name of — information in itself
- He has been performing since the last transport touched down fourteen hours ago
- He has a practiced answer and a true answer; he has decided he is done with practiced answers
- The man asks his question. Hale answers honestly.
- Frame established: Hale survived; something happened worth accounting for

---

## CHAPTER ONE — "Meridian"
**Location:** Outside Fallujah, Iraq  
**Dateline:** November 2004, 0340 local

**Beats:**
- Hale steps off the transport, stands on the strip, lets his eyes adjust
- The darkness is different — thicker, heat from the day still in it, burn pit smell upwind
- 200-meter walk to the FOB; he takes his time
- Metal door, new, security-minded — recently installed, someone thought about this doorway
- BRICK fills the doorframe: 6'3", 245lbs; the file prepared Hale for the numbers but not the fact of him
- The rank exchange: "I'm an E-9, Lieutenant. Brick's a title, not a courtesy."
- Interior of the FOB: first aid kit, water purification rig, camp stove — placed once, not intended to move
- The terminal: hardwired cable, video capability confirmed, budget that the building itself would never suggest
- ARES is released from the crate — comes out without hurry, without gratitude, with the measured deliberation of something that knew exactly what it was going to do next
- Ares checks the room, returns to Hale's left knee, sits
- Hale thinks about the authorization structure: thorough and hollow simultaneously
- He thinks about the coordinator at the safe house who spoke in the careful language of a man who chose every word the way a surgeon chooses instruments
- Outside: the generator hums. The terminal waits.

## CHAPTER TWO — "The Parish Delivers"
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** The following morning

**Beats:**
- Padre and Gremlin arrive mid-argument on a contractor flight (Beechcraft, logo half-sanded — the aviation equivalent of their documentation)
- The argument has not paused for disembarking, gear collection, or acknowledging other people
- BRICK positions left and behind — already his default geography; geography as information
- PADRE's hands: medical professional's hands, not a breacher's hands — Hale files this
- GREMLIN's handshake: calibrating before committing, testing weight before position
- Gremlin asks a question in the shape of a joke that is actually a real question
- Hale works through gear layout — methodical, meditative; finds a grain of sand in the bolt carrier
- PADRE cooks from contractor-issue MREs (rations that tasted like a committee decided what food was, described it to someone who'd never eaten) and produces something the team eats without complaint
- GREMLIN fixes the generator: 40 minutes, tool roll, 20 minutes, clean — sits back down with tea and satisfaction out of proportion to the task. Hale approves.
- CRICKET in the back room with the terminal: studying it, learning a language from its grammar before attempting to speak it
- WRAITH — present, minimal, already correctly positioned
- First package has not yet arrived

---

## CHAPTER THREE — "Pattern of Life"
**Location:** The FOB Back Room  
**Dateline:** November 2004

**Beats:**
- Cricket's POV — the chapter is his
- First package arrives; Cricket reads it twice
- First read: intelligence — biography, operational history, the target profile
- Second read: how a targeting package represents a person; the compression of a life into details useful for determining whether it should continue
- He typed four words the night after the first package arrived — never added to, never revealed
- His pen stops clicking when he finds something wrong
- He thinks about what it means to be very good at a particular kind of work, whether the quality of the analysis is separable from what it finds, whether they should be
- Pattern of Life: the biographical consistency that makes someone a valid target — Cricket reads it in the specific language of someone who knows what it is used for

---

## CHAPTER FOUR — "Ares Works"
**Location:** Fallujah Outer Districts  
**Dateline:** 0100–0430 Local

**Beats:**
- First field movement as a unit
- Moving through the Jolan district's outer edge, working east
- Agricultural structures provide cover and reference line
- To the south: orange smear of a burn pit working through the night — someone else's FOB disposing of the day's output
- WRAITH on Hale's left and slightly behind — not positioned, self-positioned
- ARES on point: shoulders forward, head slightly low, not aggressive — reading
- The unit's chemistry begins to establish itself in movement
- No contact — this is a pattern-of-life movement, intelligence-gathering
- The unit returns clean

## CHAPTER FIVE — "The First Confession"
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** Night before first operation

**Beats:**
- PADRE outside, east wall, rosary moving through his fingers
- ARES exits and sits beside him — no command, no invitation, simply present
- The man with his rosary and the dog with his sovereignty
- Padre thinks about *disponibilidad* — availability, the willingness to be found
- His eleven years of occupying rather than resolving his theological ambiguity
- GREMLIN draws a ghost on his rifle stock — no explanation given, none needed
- Cricket's four words referenced but not revealed
- The team's individual pre-operation rituals establish character without dramatization

## CHAPTER SIX — "Reaper Six, Execute"
**Location:** Fallujah, Target Area  
**Dateline:** First operation

**Beats:**
- Package 1 execution
- Clean operation — no complications documented
- Hale reads the biography section of the package afterward
- Files a question he doesn't yet have language for
- The work is done. The question is beginning.

## CHAPTER SEVEN — "What Brick Knows"
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** Post-operation

**Beats:**
- Brick tells Hale what he has been observing — not challenge, observation
- Four weeks of filing data points about how Hale processes things
- "You stopped before you were done." Not accusation. Information.
- Brick's silences are positions being taken; Hale understands this instinctively
- The two men establish their working register: respect without ceremony
- Brick does not advise. He reports. The distinction matters.

## CHAPTERS EIGHT THROUGH TEN
**Location:** The FOB / Field  
**Dateline:** November 2004

**Beats (compressed):**
- Packages 2, 3, 4 executed
- Cricket's pen stops more frequently
- Pattern becoming visible: what the packages have in common, and what they conspicuously lack
- Ch9: Cricket has underlined three things in a biography section. The paper is in his boot.
- Ch10: Something in the room changes after the fourth package. Nobody names it. Everyone registers it.
- Hale thinks about the authorization structure with increasing specificity; what the machine was built to do before they arrived at the controls

## CHAPTERS ELEVEN THROUGH FIFTEEN
**Location:** FOB / Baghdad Green Zone / Field  
**Dateline:** Late November 2004

**Beats (compressed):**
- Ch11 (Brick's chapter): Brick travels to Baghdad. Flat Iraqi desert — the city recalibrating its rhythms. He asks about methodology, operational history, whether the prior unit worked under the same informational constraints. The answer was yes.
- Brick returns. During the briefing: sits with hands flat on the table, expression communicating through what it doesn't move. He had gone to confirm something. He had confirmed it. The column was full. What came next wasn't his to call.
- Packages 5 and 6 executed
- The Confessor's phrasing shifts subtly between packages — Cricket notices
- Ch14: Cricket has been building something. It is almost complete.
- Ch15 "Two Packages. Fourteen Days.": The Architect signals the final phase. Two packages remaining. Cricket shares what he has built. The team sits with it. Nobody leaves with the same understanding they came in with.

## CHAPTER SIXTEEN — "The Picture"
**Location:** The FOB Back Room  
**Dateline:** The Following Morning [previously: The Morning After Ch15]

**Beats:**
- Cricket has not slept — not unusual, not the same as sleep deprivation
- The team assembles around what Cricket assembled
- Hale sees the full architecture for the first time
- The list was never about insurgents or weapons
- Built before the war started, by someone who needed to know what the intelligence actually showed — and needed to eliminate the people who knew
- Brick confirms (Green Zone trip): prior unit worked blind the same way. It had.
- He had not brought this detail earlier because the list had been enough for that room; he brings it now because now it's needed.

- Ch18: Gremlin asks the most important question anybody asks in the entire series

## CHAPTER TWENTY — "GREY"
**Location:** Mosul  
**Dateline:** Two Days Later [previously: Two Days After Ch19]

**Beats:**
- GREY contact through the terminal administrative interface — 0547
- Different building from previous interfaces
- GREY's corrected position on the seventh package: "I told you to look carefully at the seventh package before deciding what to do with it. You looked. You did not run it. That was the correct decision."
- GREY does not resolve anything. It confirms the mechanism is aware of itself.
- Hale: "I haven't made that decision yet. Not formally."
- The oversight mechanism that isn't oversight is now documented as having been in contact

## CHAPTER TWENTY-ONE — "The Video Call"
**Location:** The FOB Terminal Room  
**Dateline:** Three Days Later

**Beats:**
- Request arrives through standard channel: video capability confirmed, live interface requested, 1800 local
- The terminal has always had video capability — in the way a weapon has the capability of being used in more than one way
- Hale and The Architect on video for the first time
- Two performances, perfectly matched: a conversation entirely false and completely informative
- Hale performs exactly what The Architect needs to see
- The Architect performs exactly what Hale expects to see
- The warmth The Architect projects is a control mechanism — Hale performs back
- What is actually communicated is not what is said

---

## CHAPTER TWENTY-TWO — "What We Have"
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** The following day

**Beats:**
- The team has established: list predates the unit; authorization is self-authorizing; what they've been running is not what they were told
- "Can't be unbuilt" — this is what Cricket says about what they know
- The question becomes what to do with knowing it
- Gremlin: "The same things that make us good at this make us the only people who can do something about it"
- No heroic declaration. A practical assessment.

## CHAPTERS TWENTY-THREE AND TWENTY-FOUR
**Location:** The FOB / Field  
**Dateline:** Countdown to Farhat

**Beats:**
- Ch23 "Kassem": Package 7 — the second-to-last. The team executes it knowing what they know. This choice is not dramatized. It is noted, filed, carried.
- "Package 8 of 8" — Farhat is the last
- Ch24 "The Forty-Eight Hours": The countdown. Cricket's pen doesn't stop. Wraith begins building something. Nobody asks what.

## CHAPTER TWENTY-FIVE — "Farhat"
**Location:** A building not on any operational map  
**Dateline:** Final operation

**Beats:**
- Hale and Padre. No backup. Ares.
- A man who has been waiting eighteen months
- Padre's section — his history; the morning after the Khalil operation, he had built a picture that pointed at a thing he did not want to be true
- He ran it anyway then. He is running it again now.
- The operation executes. Package 8 of 8.
- The list is complete.

## CHAPTER TWENTY-SIX — "The Evidence"
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** Overnight, After Farhat [previously: Overnight, After Ch25]

**Beats:**
- Cricket has been the third man to understand the room that night TF154 assembled
- He has been running a parallel thread from his own angle
- What he has built — the full documentation — needs to survive regardless of what happens next
- The record exists. The question is whether it can be preserved.
- "Whether they should be" — Cricket's assessment is binary: file and continue

## CHAPTERS TWENTY-SEVEN AND TWENTY-EIGHT
**Location:** The FOB  
**Dateline:** Final night

**Beats:**
- Ch27: Brick sends six words. "Brick's six words had satisfied the form. The completion update would not come."
- The team is no longer running the list — they are figuring out what to do with having run it
- Ch28: WRAITH plants a cached drive. Location known only to Wraith. This matters in Book 5.
- The team does the work of people who understand they may be the last people to hold this information
- Hale leads them to the vehicle. Does not look back.

**Location:** The FOB exterior / An Unmarked Facility, Northern Virginia  
**Dateline:** February 2005 (frame closes)

**Beats:**
- Ares waits by the vehicle, facing the door
